Elevating Container Security: Leveraging the OCI Artifacts Specification

Secure Container Supply Chain: Effective management of vulnerabilities and software dependencies is crucial in bolstering container security. A significant development in this field is the adoption of standardized Software Bills of Materials (SBOMs) and the practice of signing attestations.

The introduction of the OCI artifacts specification marks a pivotal advance, offering a streamlined and robust approach that enables users to sign images, as well as store and authenticate SBOMs, scan results, provenance, and other critical supply chain attestations directly within the registry, aligning closely with the artifacts they pertain to.

During this presentation, participants will gain insights into how to approach Secure Container Supply Chain and wil be inspired to enhance their vulnerability management strategies by leveraging registry capabilities and OCI specification. They will also explore how to harness the power of open-source tools such as Notary (signing), Trivy (Vulnerability scanning and SBOM generation), ORAS (interacting with OCI registries), and Kyverno (Kubernetes Policy Engine) to optimize their container security postures.
